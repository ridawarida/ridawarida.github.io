Writing Poetry

I have been writing poetry since I was a child, and it has always been a way for me to express my thoughts and emotions. I enjoy writing about my feelings, often juxtaposing them and nature to create vivid imagery. I find that poetry allows me to explore my inner world and connect with others on a deeper level. I have shared one of my poems below:

“A Ritual of Sighs”

It is another Sunday.

I gaze out the windows

At the brooding, concrete beings towering the street.

A sky smudged the colour of an old eraser

The same cat slinks through the same alley

No words fill the blank page in front of me.

Twirling the pen in my hand, and leaning towards it,

I press its dull point against a page

As blank and expectant as the days before it.

A weary sigh leaves my lips,

But my mind knows.

It is yet another Sunday.

My chest heaves high and low;

Rhyming unevenly with

The ticking of the analog watch on my wrist–

I am but anywhere near, in my grey walled echochamber,

To the wild, wet green of a forest I’ve never seen,

To the frenzied roar of azure waters just below the ends of a hill,

To a setting sun just crossing the horizon, abashedly bowing, yet ever-changing

In its colors of lavender, orange, and periwinkle

A familiar melancholy instead

Takes its reign on my daydreams.

And so the watch will win.

The sand will drain from the glass.

Tomorrow, a grey Monday, will nudge its way in,

A carbon copy of the last.

I will reminisce, of being the protagonist of a story I never once was,

So adventurous, so full of life

While the figure in the window remains,

A quiet shell of a being,

That fades like the last glimpse of sun on those distant hills.

The pen will wait, an unhurried weight,

For another week to end,

And another Sunday to begin.
